Keith Haring

activist and artist

“Art should be something that liberates your soul, provokes the imagination and encourages people to go further.”

TIMELINE

  • 1958 - Keith Haring was born in Kutztown, Pennsylvania.
  • 1978- Keith began his studies at the School of Visual Arts in New York on a scholarship.
  • 1980- Inspired by graffiti art, Keith began making chalk drawings on blank advertising panels in New York subway stations.
  • 1981- Keith's art gained popularity and had four exhibitions of his work in New York City, and was part of eight group exhibitions in New York, London, and Belgium.
  • 1982- Printed and distributed 20,000 free posters for an anti-nuclear rally in New York City.
  • 1984- Created logo for the "Anti-Litterbug Campaign" for New York's Department of Sanitation.
  • 1985- Keith Haring designed four Swatch watches.
  • 1986- Painted a 300 foot mural on the Berlin Wall, West Germany.
  • 1986- Opened Pop Shop in New York City as a means to distribute his art and message without using traditional art distributors and galleries.
  • 1988- Created poster for National Coming Out Day.
  • 1989- The Keith Haring Foundation was created to empower children and AIDS victims.
  • 1990- Keith dies from AIDS.
